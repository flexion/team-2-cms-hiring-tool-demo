# Hypothesis

## Single-Sentence Pitch

We believe that a unified, LLM-enhanced position management app for positions ready to post will improve front-end hiring quality and reduce cycle time — by giving BOG staff rule-aware PD drafting, visual resume-to-requirements mapping, and a shared view of active postings in one place — and we'll know it's working when PD revision cycles with OHC decrease and reviewers spend less time per resume without sacrificing assessment quality.

---

## Primary Hypothesis

We believe that providing BOG/component staff with an LLM-enhanced workspace for positions at the ready-to-post stage will reduce process friction and improve hiring outcomes — because front-end quality (PDs, resume review) directly determines downstream candidate pool quality, and the tools that support this work today are fragmented, manual, and disconnected from one another.

**We'll know this is true when:**

- Time spent on PD drafting and revision cycles decreases measurably
- Users report they can hand off in-progress positions to a colleague without loss of context
- Positions managed through the tool move from posting to certificate faster than those that aren't

---

## Sub-hypothesis 1: Position Tracking (Landing Page)

We believe that a shared view of positions currently in active posting preparation will give teams coordination and visibility for the work that matters right now — because the critical window between "approved to post" and "certificate issued" is where most controllable delays and quality issues occur.

**We'll know this is true when:** team members can pick up another's in-progress position without needing a walkthrough.

---

## Sub-hypothesis 2: Working Copy Editor

We believe that an LLM writing assistant trained on PD rules and OHC conventions will reduce PD revision cycles between BOG and OHC — because most revision requests stem from non-compliance with formatting, specialized experience language norms, or classification conventions that are learnable rules, not judgment calls.

**We'll know this is true when:** PDs submitted through the tool require fewer OHC edit rounds than PDs developed without it.

---

## Sub-hypothesis 3: Resume Reader

We believe that a visual mapping tool linking PD requirements to resume content will help reviewers make faster, more consistent qualification assessments — not by scoring candidates, but by reducing the cognitive load of cross-referencing two dense documents manually.

**We'll know this is true when:** reviewers report higher confidence in their assessments AND time-per-resume decreases without a change in qualification determination accuracy.

---

## Future Sub-hypothesis: Geolocation Canary

We believe that surfacing historical posting outcomes by geolocation will enable BOG staff to avoid predictable posting failures — because repeated failures in the same market for the same role type indicate either talent pool exhaustion or PD mismatch, both of which are actionable if visible.

**We'll know this is true when:** posting failure rates decrease for positions where the canary data influenced the posting decision.
